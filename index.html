Description

Albeit tons of real-world datasets and challenges for regression and classification exists, only few are available for the task of anomaly detection. Those few, however, are primarily focused on large-scale, covariate shift, or concept drift performance.

In contrast, we propose an anomaly detection challenge for data with complex dependency structure. For that, we provide a benchmark dataset for detection of years of heavy crop yield loss in India, based upon freely available statistics. Here, anomaly detection methods are forced to leverage the inter-dependency structure of measurements.

We describe each step of the non-trivial dataset construction process in detail and further, propose an anomaly detection challenge. In a series of experiments we examine the dataset and provide some baseline benchmarks based on a variety of supervised, semi-supervised, and unsupervised machine learning methods. We invite researchers to participate and share their results.
